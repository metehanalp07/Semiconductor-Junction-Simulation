① Depletion

Diffusion of majority carriers exposes (−) acceptor & (+) donor ions. Charge neutrality: Nₐx_p=Nᴅx_n. E_bi from N→P opposes diffusion. W_d=√(2ε(Nₐ+Nᴅ)(V₀−Vₐ)/(qNₐNᴅ)). Asymmetric: wider into lighter-doped side.

② Forward Bias

Vₐ>0 opposes E_bi → barrier↓, W_d↓. Minority injection: e⁻→P, h⁺→N. J=J₀(e^(qV/kT)−1). Injected carriers decay exponentially with diffusion length L=√(Dτ). Recombination produces ✦.

③ Reverse Bias

Vₐ<0 aids E_bi → barrier↑, W_d↑. Only J₀ leakage (thermally generated minority carriers swept by E). E_max=2(V₀−Vₐ)/W_d increases. C_j=εA/W_d decreases (varactor effect).

④ Breakdown

Avalanche (N<5×10¹⁷): KE carriers impact-ionize Si → cascade. M=1/(1−(V/V_BR)ⁿ). V_BR↑ with T (more phonon scattering). Zener (N>5×10¹⁷): quantum tunneling through narrow W_d. V_BR↓ with T (Eg narrows).

⑤ Band Diagram

Ec,Ev bend creating barrier qV₀. Forward: bands flatten → injection. Reverse: steeper bending. E_F=constant at eq; splits into E_Fn, E_Fp under bias (quasi-Fermi levels). Eg=1.12eV for Si.

⑤ BJT Mode

γ=emitter injection eff. ≈1/(1+DₚNB/(DₙNE)). α_T=base transport ≈1−W_B²/(2L²). β=γα_T/(1−γα_T). Thin base W_B≪Lₙ → most injected carriers reach collector. I_C=βI_B.
